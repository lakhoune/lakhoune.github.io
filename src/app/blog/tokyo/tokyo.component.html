Tokyo: 4-day travel guide

  • Day 1: Asakusa and Ginza: Asakusa has a lot of old temples and traditional snacks. Start at Nakamise-dori street to make your way to the Asakusa shrine

    Try to get here early in the morning to avoid crowds. Shopping mall

    Go to Ueno parc. Make sure to visit the Toshogu Shrine before 16:00. Walk by the river to get a nice view of Tokyo Sky Tree, the third highest building in the world.

    In the evening, travel to Tokyo Tower.

  • Day 2: Marunouchi: In Tokyo center visit the emperors palace. Walk to the East Gardens. Walk to the Ginza district to see the famous Ginza Scramble Crossing. Nearby Revive Kitchen Three is a good vegan restaurant. LLAB is a pub where you can craft your own beer.

    In the evening, travel to Akihabara

  • Day 3: Shibuya and Harajuku. Start with Harajuku shopping district with nice vintage shops and sneaker shops. Walk to Takeshita St. (very busy). Eat vegan ramen at AFURI Harajuku.

    Walk through Yoyogi Park.

    Travel to Shibuya station. See the famous Shibuya Scramble Crossing. Get a top down view of it from Shibuya Sky (need to make reservation at least a month in advance)

    Go to Loft souvenir shop. Go to Nintendo Tokyo shop. The building has a great rooftop where you see over Shibuya.

    Go clubbing at Enter Shibuya (techno club)

  • Day 4: Nakameguro and Shinjuku

    Visit Nakameguro only during cherry blossom season. Start at Naka-Meguro Park. Walk towards Meguro river towards Naka-Meguro Koukashita shopping mall. Have the vegan curry for lunch at Alaska zwei. Visit the Sky Garden, on good days you can see the tip of Mt. Fuji

    Travel to Shinjuku, visit the National Garden and the Government building. Visit the Hanazono Shrine and walk through Golden Gai to enjoy small bars with less tourists. Visit the narrow Omoide Yokocho street (crowded, very touristy).

    Have dinner at Vegan & Wine Bar Kiboko (make reservation one day in advance). Alternatively, visit Doumo for vegan burritos
